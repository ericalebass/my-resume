What I do for fun

  • Photography: Capturing urban and natural landscapes helps me refine my understanding of composition, lighting, and visual hierarchy. This creative outlet directly informs my UX/UI work by training my eye to notice the small details that make a design "pop."
  • Open Source & Creative Coding: I enjoy tinkering with CSS and JavaScript libraries in my spare time.
  • Cooking: I view cooking as an exercise in "Back-End" preparation and "Front-End" presentation. It requires precise timing, a clear sequence of operations, and an aesthetic touch to ensure the final product is both functional and delightful.
  • Hiking and Running: Endurance activities are my go-to for mental clarity and discipline. Navigating complex trails is much like debugging code—it requires persistence, a steady pace, and the ability to pivot when you hit an unexpected obstacle.
  • Reading: I am a lifelong learner who enjoys diving into technical documentation, design case studies, and non-fiction. This habit helps me keep my communication skills polished and provides fresh perspectives on how to solve modern digital problems.
